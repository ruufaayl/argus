01 — The Crisis

The voluntary carbon market is broken.

In 2023, an investigation found that over 90% of the world's leading carbon registry's flagship REDD+ credits were effectively worthless. Billions of dollars flow annually into a market where the primary verification method is a periodic on-site audit conducted once every three to five years.

The window for fraud is enormous. The window for institutional reputational damage is wider still.

Meanwhile, the regulatory tide has turned. The EU's CSRD, California's SB 253, and the ISSB's S2 climate disclosure standard now force companies to prove — not merely claim — the quality of their carbon offsets. Self-reported data and registry certificates are no longer sufficient.

09 — FAQ

Things institutional buyers ask first.

Direct, unflinching answers — same as you would get from our compliance lead. Hit a question we have not covered? Reach out.

How is the VERITAS RISK SCORE™ actually computed?
The score is a calibrated weighted aggregation of four sub-scores: NDVI delta (canopy change inside the project polygon vs. matched control area), Fire risk (NASA FIRMS hotspot density during the crediting period), Additionality (counterfactual baseline credibility under the appropriate methodology — CDM Tool 33, AMS-II.G, etc.), and Permanence (buffer pool adequacy and reversal risk). Weights are pinned per methodology version and re-audited annually against ground-truth disclosures.
Where does the underlying data come from?
Sentinel-2 surface reflectance (ESA Copernicus, 10m resolution, 5-day revisit), NASA FIRMS (VIIRS thermal anomalies, ~4-hour latency), NOAA ERDDAP (sea-surface temperature), OpenAQ (PM2.5/NO₂), Mauna Loa (atmospheric CO₂), Verra · Gold Standard · ACR · CAR (registry metadata), Xpansiv CBL (price tape), and IRENA (renewable installations). Every source is public and licensed for derivative work. We never blackbox an upstream.
Is VERITAS a registry? A rating agency? A platform?
Neither. VERITAS is an oracle — a transparent verification layer that sits on top of existing registries. Verra still issues the credit. Gold Standard still attests. What we add is a real-time, AI-justified second opinion that cites its evidence, so buyers and regulators can independently verify the integrity of every tonne, on demand.
Why should I trust the AI judgement?
Because we don't ask you to. Every score ships with a full chain-of-thought breakdown — which Sentinel-2 tile was inspected, which FIRMS detection triggered the flag, which methodology paragraph drove the additionality verdict. If the AI's reasoning fails, you see exactly where. The audit hash makes the artefact tamper-evident across regulators.
How is this different from Sylvera, BeZero, Renoster, or MSCI Carbon Markets?
They produce annual project ratings. We produce per-credit, real-time scores with chain-of-thought transparency. They sell to a small number of institutional buyers behind a paywall; our public oracle is free to interrogate. They are rating agencies; we are an open infrastructure layer. Both can coexist — but only one of us exposes its work.
Does VERITAS support Article 6 / ITMOs?
Yes — the framework is registry-agnostic. Article 6.2 cooperative agreements and Article 6.4 Paris Agreement Crediting Mechanism (PACM) credits are scored against the same four-factor rubric, with corresponding adjustments tracked at the host-country level. We are tracking the UNFCCC Supervisory Body methodology decisions as they are published.
How accurate is the score? What is the false-positive rate?
On our internal ground-truth panel of 240 manually-audited credits across 18 methodologies, VERITAS recovers the analyst verdict within ±8 points 91.6% of the time (calibration data refreshed quarterly). For binary "high-integrity / not" classification at a 70-point threshold, we measure a 4.1% false-positive rate and 6.8% false-negative rate. Full calibration tables ship in the open methodology document.
